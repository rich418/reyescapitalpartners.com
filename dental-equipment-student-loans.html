Short Answer

With specialty equipment lenders, student loans are excluded from tradeline analysis. They look at your credit score and payment history — not your debt-to-income ratio. For most dentists and doctors, student loans are not a barrier to equipment financing.

Why student loans don't block
equipment financing

There are two ways lenders evaluate debt: as a tradeline (account history) and in DTI (debt-to-income ratio). Traditional banks use both. They'll see your $280,000 in student loans and run DTI calculations that kill your application before they even look at your credit score.

Specialty equipment lenders work differently. They underwrite against the equipment being financed — the dental chair, the X-ray unit, the CAD/CAM system — not against your personal balance sheet. The equipment is the collateral. Your professional license and credit score are the qualifying factors. Student loans are excluded from tradeline analysis in dedicated medical and dental lending programs.

This is why a dentist with $300K in student loans and a 710 FICO can get approved in 48 hours through our lender network when their bank would decline them in 10 minutes.

What Lenders Look At

Credit Score

Your FICO score and payment history. 650+ opens the core programs. 700+ unlocks the best rates (8–12% APR).

What Lenders Look At

Professional License

Your D.D.S., D.M.D., or M.D. license is a significant credentialing factor. It signals earning potential and professional stability.

What Lenders Look At

Time in Practice

2+ years = established practice, easier approval. Under 2 years = new practice program. Student loans don't change either path.

What Lenders Do NOT Use

Student Loan Balance

Student loans are excluded from tradeline analysis in dedicated medical/dental programs. The dollar amount of your loans is not a factor.

What equipment qualifies for
dental & medical financing

Any equipment used in patient care or practice operations qualifies. This includes:

Dental: Dental chairs and delivery systems, digital X-ray units, CBCT/3D imaging, CAD/CAM systems (Cerec, etc.), panoramic radiography, intraoral cameras, sterilization equipment, practice management systems, laser dentistry equipment, nitrous oxide systems.

Medical / Specialty: Exam tables, ultrasound systems, EKG/ECG equipment, surgical lights, autoclave sterilizers, physical therapy equipment, ophthalmology equipment, dermatology lasers, endoscopy equipment.

Practice buildout-related: Dental cabinetry packages, compressors, vacuum systems, and dental chair packages through authorized vendors.
