Bad Credit Equipment Loans in California: What Actually Works

A low credit score narrows your options — it doesn't eliminate them. Here's what California business owners need to know.

By Oscar Reyes · Reyes Capital Partners Ventura, CA 7 min read

If your credit score is below 650 and you've tried to finance equipment through a bank, you already know the answer you get. What most people don't know is how different the options are once you step outside the traditional bank channel.

This guide breaks down what "bad credit" actually means for equipment financing in California, which lenders work in this space, and what you can realistically expect in terms of approval, rates, and terms.

What Lenders Look at Beyond Your Score

A credit score is one data point. For equipment financing specifically, lenders also weigh:

The Equipment's Collateral Value

A 2021 John Deere excavator is worth $85,000 on the open market. If you stop paying, the lender takes the machine back and sells it. That recovery option changes the risk profile significantly — it's why equipment loans are often easier to get than unsecured business loans, even with challenged credit.

Recent Payment History

A bankruptcy from four years ago matters less than how you've paid your bills for the last 12 months. Lenders who work in this space understand that credit scores are backward-looking. Consistent recent payment history — even on just utilities, phone bills, and a credit card — tells a cleaner story than the score number alone.

Time in Business

Startups with bad credit face a harder road than established businesses with bad credit. If you've been operating for 2+ years, that history helps offset a low score. Even one year gives you something to work with.

Industry and Equipment Type

Construction equipment, commercial trucks, and agricultural equipment all have strong secondary markets. Lenders are more comfortable with equipment that holds its value. Specialty or niche equipment is harder to finance on challenged credit because recovery is less certain.

The cleaner the equipment, the easier the deal. A newer machine with a strong resale market gives a lender a safety net — and that safety net is what gets challenged-credit deals approved.

The Two Types of Lenders That Work With Bad Credit

Banks & Credit Unions

  • 650–720+ FICO required

  • Lowest rates — if you qualify

Specialty / Alt Lenders

  • 580–640+ FICO considered

  • App-only up to $75K

  • Higher rates — but accessible

What to Expect on Rates and Terms

Honesty matters here: challenged-credit equipment financing is more expensive than prime financing. Here's the realistic range:

  • 580–619 FICO: 18–28% factor rate range, 24–60 month terms
  • 620–649 FICO: 14–22% factor rate range, 36–60 month terms
  • 650–679 FICO: 10–16% factor rate range, 48–72 month terms

The right question isn't "what's the rate?" — it's "does the equipment generate more than it costs?" A skid steer that lets you take $15,000/month in additional grading contracts is worth financing at 22% interest. The math works. Waiting for your credit to improve costs more in lost revenue than any rate differential.

How to Maximize Your Chances

  1. Don't apply to banks first. Hard inquiries from declines drop your score 5–15 points each. If your score is already borderline, a string of bank declines can knock you out of specialty lender range too. Come to a broker who knows this space before you apply anywhere.
  2. Know your equipment cold. Year, make, model, hours/mileage, condition, and the seller's name and phone number. Lenders who work fast need this information instantly — not in a follow-up email three days later.
  3. Have a clean 12-month payment story. If you've paid rent, utilities, and any credit obligations on time for the last year, make sure that shows up in your application conversation. Recent history matters more than old events.
  4. Don't overborrow. If a lender will approve $75K but you need $50K, take the $50K. A lower loan amount reduces lender risk and makes approval more likely on marginal credit.
  5. Work with a broker, not just one lender. One lender's decline is another's approval. A broker with relationships across 4–8 lenders can match your profile to the right box without burning inquiries on the wrong ones.

The Section 179 Angle

California business owners who finance equipment before December 31, 2026 can deduct up to $1.22 million from their 2026 federal taxes under Section 179 — even if the equipment is financed. You don't have to pay cash to get the write-off. For a contractor in a 22% tax bracket financing a $70,000 excavator, that's roughly $15,400 back at tax time. Talk to your CPA, but this is real money and it applies even to challenged-credit borrowers.

A bad credit score today doesn't mean a bad credit score forever. Every on-time payment on your equipment loan builds the credit history that makes your next financing cheaper. This loan is the foundation, not the ceiling.
