Why Banks Say No

Banks aren't saying no to you.
They're saying no to your category.

Banks have rigid underwriting boxes. If you're outside the box — too new, too thin a credit file, too much existing debt, wrong industry — they decline automatically. It's not personal. It's a scoring model that doesn't know your business, your jobs lined up, or your cash flow.

Here's what most banks require that equipment lenders don't:

Bank Requirement

2+ Years in Business

Banks rarely lend to businesses under 2 years old regardless of credit or cash flow. Equipment lenders look at the deal differently — the equipment itself is collateral.

Bank Requirement

680+ Credit Score

Most bank equipment loan programs require 680+ minimum. Equipment finance lenders specialize in programs starting at 550 — with rates that reflect the risk, not a hard no.

Bank Requirement

Tax Returns & Financials

Banks require 2 years of tax returns and business financials. Equipment lenders can approve deals under $75K with just an application and equipment invoice.

Bank Requirement

Weeks to Decide

Bank equipment loan reviews take 2–6 weeks. Equipment finance decisions come back in 24 hours. If you have a job lined up, you can't wait six weeks for a bank to review your file.
